Our corner

The old hardware store, reborn as the neighborhood's living room.

In 2019, Nora & Eli Hartman took the keys to a 1920s hardware store on Foundry Street — pressed-tin ceiling, worn plank floors and all — and never quite finished tearing out the old shelving. They liked the way it held a room together.

They put a small-batch roaster where the paint counter used to be, a griddle where the register once sat, and started pulling shots for whoever wandered in. Word got around the way it does in a good neighborhood — slowly, then all at once.

Today the same regulars turn up before sunrise for the first pour, students camp out by the windows, and the patio fills with dogs and strollers by mid-morning. It's less a coffee shop than a place the whole block happens to share.

— Nora & Eli
